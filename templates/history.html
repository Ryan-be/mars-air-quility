Compares indoor sensor temperature against outdoor weather data. Large gaps suggest poor insulation or ventilation. The outdoor trace is dashed and comes from your configured weather location.
Compares indoor and outdoor humidity. If indoor humidity stays high while outdoor is low, your room may lack ventilation. Conversely, indoor humidity dropping below outdoor suggests dehumidification or heating effects.
Absolute humidity measures the actual water content in grams per cubic metre of air. Unlike relative humidity, it doesn't depend on temperature. Values above 12 g/m³ may encourage mould growth.
The dew point is the temperature at which moisture will condense. When the air temperature drops close to the dew point (margin < 3°C), condensation can form on cold surfaces — risking mould and water damage.
Shows when the fan was on (1) or off (0), derived from smart plug power readings. Correlate this with temperature and TVOC charts to verify the fan is activating when needed.
Vapour Pressure Deficit (VPD) measures the drying power of air. It combines temperature and humidity into a single metric critical for plant health. Low VPD (< 0.4 kPa) means high mould risk; ideal vegetative growth is 0.8–1.2 kPa; above 1.6 kPa causes plant stress.
